Standards

The Rerum Consortium seeks out consensus great standards for Digital Humanities and illuminates the areas where none seem to exist. Where possible, the membership will extend existing models, organize a critical mass of use cases, and map the required work for developers and programmers.

Text

There have been great strides made in defining interoperable character sets and fonts for digital display across many languages. Encoding formatted and annotated text has been possible in SGML, XML, HTML, LaTeX, markdown, and others. Standards for these formats include TEI, but are not as well defined. This is one of the first areas the Consortium hopes to attack and make these text resources more annotatable, referencable, and durable.

Image

IIIF is the flagship here. Not only does it serve as a model for other areas, but describes standards for both the presentation and delivery of image-based resources. There is still room for improvement regarding the available formats of terminal image resources, but there is much to be lauded in the status quo.

Audio

MEI is a start, but there is very little done to extend OAC annotations into the realm of audio resoures.
